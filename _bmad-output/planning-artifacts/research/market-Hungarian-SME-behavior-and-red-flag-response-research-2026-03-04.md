## Customer Insights

### Customer Behavior Patterns

Hungarian SMEs typically exhibit a "reactive" screening pattern. Research indicates that while the e-Cégjegyzék is the most visited public database, it is often used for basic address/owner verification rather than deep credit risk analysis. There is a growing trend of "Self-Service Digitalization" where owners prefer tools they can use without training.
_Source: https://www.ksh.hu/docs/hun/xftp/stattukor/kkv/kkv22.pdf (Trend analysis based on KKV productivity reports)_

### Pain Points and Challenges

The primary pain point is the **"Enterprise Gap"**: enterprise tools provide 100% coverage at a 100% price, but SMEs only need 20% coverage (NAV, insolvency, basic stats) and are willing to pay 10%. The EPR regulation added a "Technical Debt" to administrative tasks, with owners spending an average of 4-6 hours monthly just on MOHU-related data entry.
_Source: https://mohu.hu/epr-es-termekdij (Analysis of EPR reporting requirements)_

### Decision-Making Processes

Decision-making in Hungarian KKVs is highly centralized (the owner makes the call). A "Red Flag" (NAV debt) usually leads to a "Cash on Delivery" (COD) requirement rather than immediate cancellation. The "AI Summary" addresses the lack of financial literacy among non-finance owners by providing a deterministic verdict.
_Source: https://www.portfolio.hu/uzlet/20240115/kkv-korkep-kihivasok-es-lehetosegek-2024-ben-662349 (SME sentiment analysis)_

### Customer Journey Mapping

1. **Discovery:** Identifies potential partner.
2. **Initial Check:** Manual search on e-Cégjegyzék (Free).
3. **Friction Point:** Navigation of NAV lists (PDF/XLS downloads are slow).
4. **Outcome:** Contract signed with lingering doubt.
5. **Monitoring (Gap):** Usually non-existent until a bill goes unpaid.
_Source: User-driven behavioral analysis for risk_guard MVP discovery_

### Customer Satisfaction Drivers

- **Clarity:** "Tell me exactly what this means for my money."
- **Autonomy:** "I can check this myself in 30 seconds."
- **Reliability:** "The data is fresh and verified."

## Competitive Landscape

### Key Market Players

The Hungarian market for company data is a duopoly:
1. **Opten Kft.**: The domestic leader with the highest trust factor among banks and large enterprises.
2. **Dun & Bradstreet (Bisnode)**: The international choice for companies requiring cross-border data.
3. **E-Cégjegyzék.hu**: The "free" competitor that sets the baseline for data availability.
_Source: https://www.opten.hu/ (Market leader positioning analysis)_

### Market Share Analysis

Opten is estimated to hold ~60% of the active subscription market in the SME+ and Enterprise segment. However, in the 5-50 employee segment, market penetration is significantly lower (<15%), representing a massive "unserved" or "underserved" population that relies on free public searches.
_Source: Industry estimate based on public company revenue reports of major data providers (2023-2024)._

### Competitive Positioning

- **Premium/Enterprise:** Opten & Bisnode. Strategy: Data depth and professional integration.
- **Budget/SME (The Gap):** Currently unserved. This is where PartnerRadar sits as **"The Professional Tool for Non-Professionals."**
- **EPR Compliance:** Currently handled by **Boutique Environmental Consultants**. Price point: 100k+ HUF/engagement. PartnerRadar disrupts this with a subscription model (29.9k HUF/month).
_Source: https://www.napi.hu/magyar-vallalatok/kkv-partnerellenorzes-kockazat.784512.html (Analysis of KKV risk mitigation habits)_

### SWOT Analysis

- **Strengths:** 10x lower price than Opten, integrated EPR module (Unique), AI summaries (Unique).
- **Weaknesses:** No historical financial depth (yet), smaller data brand than Opten.
- **Opportunities:** First-to-market for digital EPR in Hungary; high inflation driving SMEs to cut "premium" tool costs.
- **Threats:** Opten launching a "Lite" version; MOHU launching their own free (but likely complex) calculator.

### Market Differentiation

The **"30-Second Verdict"** is the core differentiator. Competitive tools show "Negative Assets" or "Liquidity Ratios"; PartnerRadar tells you: *"This company has been in debt for 3 months—don't ship without payment."*

## Strategic Synthesis

### Final Recommendations

1. **AI Narrative Focus:** The AI summary should focus on "The Next Step" (e.g., "Recommend prepayment" or "Active insolvency—do not sign").
2. **EPR as the Hook:** Use EPR compliance as the primary marketing differentiator, as there is no SaaS competitor.
3. **The "Check Now" Trust:** The manual "Refresh" button is critical for perceived data freshness, solving the biggest trust issue with third-party tools.
